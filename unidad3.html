3.1 INTERNAL FACTORS

1. Age

  • Age influences how you learn, not whether you can learn.
  • Children: better pronunciation and naturalness; they learn unconsciously.
  • Adolescents and adults: learn vocabulary and grammar faster due to greater cognitive ability.

2. Aptitude (Language ability)

The natural ability to learn languages. Includes sensitivity to sounds and grammatical patterns. People with high aptitude learn faster even with little exposure.

3. Motivation

One of the most decisive factors. High motivation = more persistence and success.

  • Integrative: Wanting to connect with culture/people.
  • Instrumental: Learning for a goal (work, school).

4. Attitudes

Positive attitudes reduce anxiety and increase willingness to speak. Negative attitudes cause resistance.

5. Learning Styles

Preferences for processing information:

  • Visual: Images, reading.
  • Auditory: Listening, songs.
  • Kinesthetic: Moving, acting.
  • Analytical: Rules, grammar.
  • Global: Meaning, communication.

3.2 EXTERNAL FACTORS

1. Learning Context & Instruction

Includes home, school, and immersion environments. Clear objectives and meaningful activities are key.

2. Input & Opportunities

Input must be Comprehensible (i+1): slightly above the learner's level. Students need opportunities to use the language (Role plays, games).
