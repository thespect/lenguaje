3.1 INTERNAL FACTORS

1. Age

  • Children: better pronunciation and naturalness; they learn unconsciously[cite: 259].

2. Aptitude (Language ability)

The natural ability to learn languages. Includes sensitivity to sounds and grammatical patterns. [cite_start]People with high aptitude learn faster even with little exposure [cite: 261-267].

3. Motivation

One of the most decisive factors. High motivation = more persistence and success.

  • Integrative: Wanting to connect with culture/people.
    • [cite_start]
  • Instrumental: Learning for a goal (work, school) [cite: 270-271].

4. Attitudes

Positive attitudes reduce anxiety and increase willingness to speak. [cite_start]Negative attitudes cause resistance [cite: 274-276].

5. Learning Styles

Preferences for processing information:

  • Visual: Images, reading.
  • Auditory: Listening, songs.
  • Kinesthetic: Moving, acting.
  • Analytical: Rules, grammar.
    • [cite_start]
  • Global: Meaning, communication [cite: 281-285].

3.2 EXTERNAL FACTORS

1. Learning Context & Instruction

Includes home, school, and immersion environments. [cite_start]Clear objectives and meaningful activities are key [cite: 327-337].

2. Input & Opportunities

Input must be Comprehensible (i+1): slightly above the learner's level. [cite_start]Students need opportunities to use the language (Role plays, games) [cite: 340-349].
